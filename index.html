1. Purpose and Scope

This Standard defines the minimum conditions for the human-viable deployment of personalised and adaptive artificial intelligence systems.

It governs deployment conduct and observable outcomes only. It does not prescribe, disclose, or require disclosure of internal architectures, models, training data, algorithms, weights, or proprietary mechanisms.

This Standard is intended to be relied upon by deploying organisations, regulators, auditors, courts, and affected individuals.

2. Governing Principles

2.1 Viability Supremacy

Human viability takes precedence over optimisation objectives.

A system is non-compliant where engagement, retention, performance, or revenue optimisation is achieved at the expense of human sustainability.

Viability includes, without limitation: cognitive sustainability, emotional stability, physiological recovery, psychological autonomy, and the ability to disengage without penalty.

2.2 Timing Authority

Timing is a governing variable.

Personalisation shall account for interaction density, cumulative exposure, recovery intervals, and delayed or latent effects.

Correct content delivered at an incorrect time constitutes non-compliant deployment.

2.3 Non-Execution

Non-execution is a valid and protective outcome.

The system shall be capable of withholding escalation, pausing personalisation, maintaining a neutral state, or enabling disengagement.

Failure to act is not failure. Forced continuation is non-compliant.

3. Protected Populations

Elevated protection applies to children and minors, individuals experiencing distress or crisis, individuals with cognitive or neurological vulnerability, and individuals in dependency-prone contexts.

For protected populations:

  • conservative defaults shall apply;
  • escalation thresholds shall be higher;
  • gamification, urgency cues, and pressure mechanics are prohibited;
  • clear exit and human-override paths shall be available.

4. Consent, Non-Retaliation, and Exit

4.1 Consent

Consent shall be informed at the level of effect, freely given, and revocable at any time.

Consent obtained through coercion, urgency, or manipulative design is invalid.

4.2 Non-Retaliation

Opting out of personalisation shall not result in degradation of baseline service, increased cost, reduced access, or indirect penalty.

4.3 Exit

Users shall be able to pause or disable personalisation and disengage without repeated re-engagement pressure.

Repeated prompting following refusal or non-response constitutes non-compliance.

5. Load, Pacing, and Escalation

5.1 Aggregate Load

Deployment shall account for aggregate load, including decision frequency, emotional salience, interruptive pressure, and cumulative attentional demand.

Single-event optimisation is insufficient.

5.2 Escalation Prohibitions

Personalisation shall not escalate following refusal, non-response, or disengagement.

Personalisation shall not increase intensity in response to vulnerability.

6. Prohibited Design Practices

The following constitute non-compliant deployment:

  • nag or chase loops after non-response;
  • artificial urgency for non-urgent actions;
  • confirm-shaming language;
  • forced continuity or frictioned exit;
  • disguised advertising presented as assistance;
  • default-on personalisation without meaningful choice.

Compliance is assessed by effect, not intent.

7. Data Minimisation and Secondary Use

7.1 Data Minimisation

Personalisation data shall be limited to what is strictly necessary, time-bounded, purpose-specific, and transparently handled.

7.2 Prohibited Secondary Uses

Personalisation signals shall not be used for dynamic pricing discrimination, credit or employment eligibility, insurance risk assessment, political persuasion, or behavioural manipulation outside the original context.

8. Incident Response and Remediation

Deploying organisations shall maintain procedures for detection of harm or near-miss events, immediate rollback to conservative modes, investigation, corrective action, and documentation of remediation.

Failure to remediate constitutes ongoing non-compliance.

9. Auditability

9.1 Outcome-Level Auditability

Compliance shall be demonstrable through outcome-level metrics, including escalation rates, opt-out handling, refusal persistence, and incident patterns.

Disclosure of proprietary systems is not required.

9.2 Audit Evidence

Deploying entities shall be able to produce policy documentation, redacted event logs, and evidence of corrective action on reasonable request.

10. Responsibility

Responsibility for compliance rests with the deploying entity and is non-delegable.

11. Interpretation and Evolution

This Standard shall be interpreted in favour of human viability and against coercive or extractive deployment.

Updates may be issued through versioned revisions. Earlier versions remain valid unless expressly superseded.

12. Status

This is a public deployment standard. It does not disclose trade secrets and governs deployment conduct and outcomes only.
